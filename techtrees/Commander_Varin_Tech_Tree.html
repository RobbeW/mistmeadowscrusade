Tech Tree Rules

  • At every tier you can select one of two possible abilities.
  • Click on each branch and tier to see more details.

Light Path

Tier 1

Lore description:

What they lack in firepower, they make up for in volume


In-Game Effect:

[infantry] and [mounted] units with this upgrade may add 4” to ranged weapons in a turn in which they Remained Stationary.

Tier 2

Lore description:

Infantrymen and -women often serve shortly on the front, though every round fired is one step to victory.


In-Game Effect:

When an [infantry] or [mounted] model from your army is slain by a ranged attack, roll a d6. On a 6+ they may shoot with a ballistic skill of 5+.


Restrictions:
You may not use attacks with the [hazardous] special rule.


Upgrade Path:
Upgraded from 'Organised Firing Lines'.

Lore description:

It takes heroes to hold the line in the face of a heretic charge. To lose such heroes enrages their comrades in arms.


In-Game Effect:

During the combat phase, if a unit is above half strength, add 1 the weapon skills of melee weapons.

Upgrade Path:
Upgraded from 'Organised Firing Lines'.

Tier 3

Lore description:

Rigorous training is a luxury rarely given to those whose home is the battlefield, though experience is a masterful teacher.


In-Game Effect:

[infantry] and [mounted] units from your army with this tech may reroll a single hit roll and a single wound roll each turn.

Lore description:

In the face of mass casualties, it takes a certain kind of madness to stand and fight twice as hard.


In-Game Effect:

If a unit is below half strength, add 1 to the attacks characteristic of melee weapons.


Tier 4

Lore description:

Even weak weapons may punch above their weight when firing upon the weaker points of enemy armor.


In-Game Effect:

Ranged weapons for your [Infantry] and [mounted] units gain the [anti-infantry 4+] keyword. If they have the [devastating wounds] keyword, they lose this keyword.

Upgrade Path:
Upgraded from 'Certified Marksmen'.

Lore description:

Infantry on the move may lack the firepower to take on heavier targets. Specialists fill this role instead.


In-Game Effect:

When selecting this tech, you may deploy a single Imperial Guard heavy weapons squad with a loadout to your liking.

Upgrade Path:
Upgraded from 'Certified Marksmen'.

Lore description:

No weapons are quite as sharp as the fire- forged and battle-tested Catachan blades.


In-Game Effect:

In a turn when an [infantry] or [mounted] unit from your army charged, melee weapons they are equipped with gain the [lethal hits] and [sustained hits 1] keywords.

Upgrade Path:
Upgraded from 'Balls of Plasteel'.

Lore description:

It takes a good officer to spur his men into a frenzy, though a spurred solider is a fearsome foe


In-Game Effect:

Each character in your army gains the following ability: “Once per battle, during the charge phase, you may reroll the charge roll for this character’s unit, and add 3” to the charge roll”

Upgrade Path:
Upgraded from 'Balls of Plasteel'.

Tier 5

Lore description:

When facing the adversary on the battlefield, taking key objectives may be the difference between victory or defeat.


In-Game Effect:

Ranged weapons equipped by [Infantry] and [mounted] units from your army gain the [assault] keyword. When advancing units with this tech, do not roll a d6. They may advance up to 6” instead.



Lore description:

There are times when heavy firepower still is relative. In such cases, heavier is always a viable option.


In-Game Effect:

When choosing this tech, you may deploy an Imperial Guard heavy mortar unit or an Imperial Guard thudd gun unit.



Lore description:

Often used by traitors and scum, stims are seen as a liability … until the local hospitaller signs off on them of course.


In-Game Effect:

Units with this tech may increase the strength and AP of their melee weapons by 1. This stacks with any other abilities the unit may have.



Lore description:

There is no weapon more reliable than the humble bayonet, and no metal holier than auramite. To combine them is to put the Emperor’s power in an infantryman’s hands.


In-Game Effect:

[Infantry] and [mounted] units in your army gain the following ability: “During the combat phase, when this unit has charged, roll 1 d6 for every model in this unit. For each 5+, the enemy receives 1 mortal wound. Add +1 to this roll if the enemy unit size is bigger than or equal to your unit size”



Tier 6

Lore description:

There are stories of ancient Terra, where ancient emperors where flanked by immortal men, whose might and bravery surpassed all.


In-Game Effect:

[Infantry] and [mounted] units from your army can only be wounded on a 3+. They also gain a 'feel no pain 5+', or increase their feel no pain rolls by 1 if they already have this ability.




Heavy Path

Tier 1

Lore description:

Heavy vehicles traverse rough paths, often covered in enemies dead and injured


In-Game Effect:

Increase the armour penetration characteristic of a [vehicle] unit’s melee attack by 1.

Tier 2

Lore description:

Battles fought on the ground are often won in orbit, where no hidey hole is safe from prying scanners and trained eyes


In-Game Effect:

During the first battle round, weapons with the [indirect fire] special rule ignore the penalty to AP when firing indirectly.

Upgrade Path:
Upgraded from 'Hardened Suspension'.

Lore description:

On a rolling mass of impenetrable plasteel and indomitable auramite, sensitive systems are a marksman’s white whale.


In-Game Effect:

When a [vehicle] unit from your army is damaged to the point of being ‘bracketed’, its main weapon does not suffer the -1 to hit penalty.

Upgrade Path:
Upgraded from 'Hardened Suspension'.

Tier 3

Lore description:

There are many ways to load an artillery shell, though few provide a show quite like burning retribution


In-Game Effect:

[indirect fire] weapons in your army can use this special rule once per game. Add the [ignores cover] and [anti-infantry 3+] special rule for that shooting phase before declaring attacks.

Lore description:

A tank crew is only as powerful as its weakest link. Young crewmen are often thoroughly tested to reinforce their resolve.


In-Game Effect:

When an enemy unit is hit by a sponson, hull- or pintle-mounted weapon, you may reroll hit rolls of 1 for the main weapon of your [vehicle].


Tier 4

Lore description:

There’s a great risk in being a stationary target. The best ways to avoid being fired upon is simply not to get spotted.


In-Game Effect:

Units with [indirect fire] weapons from your army gain the [stealth] special rule.

Upgrade Path:
Upgraded from 'Phosphorous Warheads'.

Lore description:

In the chorus of a battlefield, the thundering roar of artillery means death and destruction. The louder the men can make it roar, the happier they are.


In-Game Effect:

When rolling the dice to determine the amount of attacks on an [indirect fire] weapon, a roll of 1 or 2 count as 3.

Upgrade Path:
Upgraded from 'Phosphorous Warheads'.

Lore description:

Tanks are often slow rumbling monsters. Any commander mad enough to drive at high speeds is sure to confuse their adersary.


In-Game Effect:

Choose one weapon profile on any [vehicle] unit in your army. That weapon profile gains the [assault] keyword.

Upgrade Path:
Upgraded from 'Assistant Gunner Training'.

Lore description:

When engaging other tanks, it is of the highest importance to equip adequate protection.


In-Game Effect:

[Vehicle] units in your army gain a -1 to wound and a 6+ invulnerable save.

Upgrade Path:
Upgraded from 'Assistant Gunner Training'.

Tier 5

Lore description:

Even in an open field, a clever commander can deploy artillery pieces with relative safety. Many men may die to allow the Emperor’s divine touch to rain down from above.


In-Game Effect:

Units equipped with [indirect fire] weapons have a 5+ invulnerable save as long as they have not moved this battle.



Lore description:

Though artillery is the pinnacle of ranged warfare, there are always ways to fire upon even further targets.


In-Game Effect:

If a unit with an [indirect fire] weapon from your army has Remained Stationary, add half of the weapon’s range characteristic to the range of that weapon (E.G. 48” + 24”)



Lore description:

Some officers prefer to send infantry first into the fray to fill the gap with bodies and bullets. The smart officer sends in an armored spearhead.


In-Game Effect:

[Vehicle] units from your army that moved during this turn count as having Remained Stationary. Units embarked in [transport] units may still disembark, though no longer charge (regardless of any other rules like assault ramps)



Lore description:

A good tank commander not only knows when to order is men to fire, he knows when to aid in taking out the adversaries threatening his crew.


In-Game Effect:

If a [vehicle] unit in your army can take a pintle-mounted heavy stubber or storm bolter, you may replace it with one of the following instead and you may fire it twice per turn:

  • plasma gun
  • hotshot volleygun
  • flamer
  • grenade launcher
  • grav-gun
  • meltagun



Tier 6

Lore description:

When the full might of an armored battalion arrives on the battlefield, there is little an unprepared foe can do.


In-Game Effect:

[vehicle] units in your army that have moved less than half their movement characteristic may fire their weapons twice.

Designer's Commentary: if you have the rubber-coated roadwheels tech, the vehicle still counts as having moved however many inches when determining this tech’s effect.
